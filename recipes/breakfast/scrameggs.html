Smooth Scrambled Eggs

The best recipe by far...

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Butter
  • Salt

Optional Ingredients

  • Pepper

Preparation

Note: The more effort you put into stirring the eggs while cooking the better they turn out.

I prefer to wip up the eggs in a cold frying pan. It only works if you have a stove top that heats up quickly (Gas or Induction). Otherwise wip up your eggs in a separat bowl. Don't add any other ingredients, they come later.

Add the eggs to the frying pan. Stir constantly and quickly over low to med heat.

At about a minute or so in, when the eggs are still mostly water-like, remove the pan from the heat and add butter in thin slices (more tastes better). Return to heat and melt the butter in, stirring vigorously.

When the eggs get closer to thick mud in nature, remove from the heat and add salt to tast. (Salt changes the way the eggs gel up when cooking, so add salt as late as possible.) Return to heat and finish cooking.

Good srcambled eggs should be just past boogery (I know...) but not quite cured jello. If they respond like rubber... You have gone too far.

Enjoy!
